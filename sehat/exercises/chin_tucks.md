## Steps
1. Sit or stand tall, looking straight ahead
2. Slowly draw your chin straight back (making a "double chin")
3. Hold for 5 seconds — you should feel a gentle stretch at the base of your skull
4. Return to starting position
5. Repeat 10 times

## Tips
💡 Imagine a string pulling the top of your head toward the ceiling.
💡 Keep your eyes level — don't look up or down.

## Why It Helps
Chin tucks strengthen the deep neck flexors that support your cervical spine. They counteract the forward head posture caused by looking at screens all day. For C2-C3 radiculopathy, chin tucks open the neural foramina (nerve tunnels) — the same reason flexion/chin-to-chest feels relieving. This is the **#1 exercise** for your condition.

⚠️ Don't tilt head up or down. Movement is purely backward.
