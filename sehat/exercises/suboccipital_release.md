## Steps
1. Sit in a chair, look straight ahead
2. Place both hands behind your head, fingers interlocked at the base of your skull
3. Find the two bony bumps behind your ears — your thumbs should rest just below them in the soft tissue
4. Apply gentle, steady pressure with your thumbs into that soft spot
5. Hold 30 seconds while breathing slowly — you'll feel tightness releasing
6. Slowly nod your head forward (tiny movement) while maintaining thumb pressure
7. Hold the nod for 10 seconds, then release everything
8. Repeat 3 times

## Tips
💡 The pressure should feel like "good pain" — firm but not sharp.
💡 You may feel the jaw/behind-ear tension release during this. That's the suboccipital muscles letting go.
💡 Best done with a warm neck (after heat therapy or a hot shower).

## Why It Helps
The suboccipital muscles (at the skull base) are the most overworked muscles in computer users. They shorten to hold your head in forward posture, and when they tighten they compress the greater occipital nerve — causing referred pain to the jaw, behind the ear, and temple on the right side. This self-release technique directly decompresses that nerve. It's the single best move for the jaw/ear pain pattern.

⚠️ Do NOT press on the spine itself (center of neck). Stay on the soft tissue to the sides of the spine, just below the skull ridge.
