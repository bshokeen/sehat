## Steps
1. Sit back in your chair — let the backrest support you
2. Plant both feet flat on the floor
3. Roll your shoulders back and down
4. Align ears over shoulders, shoulders over hips
5. Gently engage your core
6. Take 2-3 deep breaths in this position

## Tips
💡 Your screen should be at eye level — if you're looking down, raise it.
💡 Elbows at ~90° angle, wrists neutral on keyboard.

## Why It Helps
Poor posture shifts your head forward, adding up to 40 lbs of extra stress on your cervical spine. Regular resets prevent the muscles from "locking" into a hunched position.
