## Steps
1. Sit upright, look straight ahead
2. Slowly tilt your head to the **LEFT** (away from the affected right side)
3. Extend your **RIGHT** arm down and slightly out, palm facing away from you
4. Slowly flex your wrist back (fingers pointing up) — feel a gentle nerve stretch
5. Hold 3 seconds, then relax the wrist and bring head back to center
6. Repeat 5 times

## Tips
💡 This should feel like a gentle pull along the nerve path — NOT sharp pain.
💡 Start very gently. Increase range over days, not minutes.
💡 If tingling increases, reduce the range — you've gone too far.

## Why It Helps
Neural glides gently mobilize the C2-C3 nerve root that's being irritated by cervical spondylosis. Instead of stretching the nerve (which can worsen irritation), gliding slides it back and forth through the foraminal tunnel, reducing adhesions and improving nerve mobility. This directly targets the right-side jaw/behind-ear referred pain and the "nerve pull" sensation.

⚠️ STOP immediately if tingling worsens or spreads into fingers. This means the nerve is being compressed, not glided.
